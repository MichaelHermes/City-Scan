How COVID risk is determined:

A state or county’s overall risk level takes into account three metrics: daily new cases per 100K (incidence), infection rate (Rt), and test positivity.

Daily new cases, also known as “incidence” in epidemiology, is the number of new COVID cases per day per unit of population.

Infection rate is the estimated number of new people each COVID-positive person will infect.

Positive test rate is the percentage of COVID testing that comes back positive.
